======================================================================
SCORE JUSTIFICATIONS
======================================================================

--- Ridge ---
  Interpretability (5/5):
    Fully interpretable: linear coefficients directly map to feature effects. Each coefficient quantifies the expected change in fucosylation per unit change in the standardized feature.
  Regulatory (4/5):
    Strong: Linear models are well-understood by regulators. Easy to validate. Limited by inability to capture nonlinear effects present in bioprocessing.
  Uncertainty (2/5):
    Prediction intervals available via OLS theory, but assumes homoscedasticity and normality — often violated in bioprocessing data.

--- PLSR ---
  Interpretability (5/5):
    Fully interpretable: VIP scores rank feature importance; latent variables provide dimensionality reduction with clear loadings. Standard in chemometrics.
  Regulatory (5/5):
    Excellent: Gold standard in PAT/QbD literature. Directly cited in regulatory guidance. Widely accepted in pharma submissions.
  Uncertainty (2/5):
    Bootstrap-based intervals possible but not built-in. Requires additional implementation for uncertainty estimation.

--- Random Forest ---
  Interpretability (3/5):
    Partially interpretable: MDI importance available but biased (Strobl et al., 2007). Permutation importance corrects this. Individual tree paths not easily inspectable.
  Regulatory (3/5):
    Moderate: Accepted in some regulatory filings but requires additional justification. Feature importance may be questioned.
  Uncertainty (3/5):
    Tree variance provides crude uncertainty (inter-tree disagreement). Not formally calibrated. Quantile regression forests improve this.

--- XGBoost ---
  Interpretability (4/5):
    Interpretable via SHAP: Lundberg & Lee (2017) provide theoretically grounded, locally faithful explanations. Interaction detection available.
  Regulatory (4/5):
    Good: With SHAP explainability, meets FDA AI/ML guidance requirements. Individual prediction explanations satisfy case-by-case justification.
  Uncertainty (2/5):
    No built-in uncertainty. Quantile regression or conformal prediction can be added but requires additional implementation.

--- GPR ---
  Interpretability (3/5):
    Limited direct interpretability but provides uncertainty bounds. Kernel hyperparameters reveal length scales (feature sensitivity). Uncertainty is itself a form of interpretability.
  Regulatory (5/5):
    Excellent: Uncertainty quantification directly supports ICH Q8 Design Space definition. Probabilistic predictions enable risk-based decision making (ICH Q9).
  Uncertainty (5/5):
    Built-in, principled uncertainty via posterior predictive distribution. Calibration verified. σ directly interpretable as prediction confidence.

--- ANN ---
  Interpretability (2/5):
    Low intrinsic interpretability. LIME provides post-hoc local explanations but global understanding is limited. Regulatory bodies may require additional validation (FDA AI guidance, 2023).
  Regulatory (2/5):
    Limited: Difficult to validate per ICH Q2. Black-box nature conflicts with regulatory expectation of process understanding. LIME helps but isn't sufficient for full regulatory compliance without extensive additional validation.
  Uncertainty (1/5):
    No built-in uncertainty. MC-Dropout or ensemble methods can approximate it but add significant complexity and aren't principled.
